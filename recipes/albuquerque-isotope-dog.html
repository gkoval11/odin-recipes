Albuquerque Isotopes Dog

Image source: Reddit

Description:

Just got off a 12 day hungry strike over your local baseball team moving cities? What better to break your fast than with an isotope dog supreme (a.k.a an Albuquerque Isotopes dog). This hot dog combines mesquite grilled onions, jalapeño relish, and mango lime salsa in this bold Southwestern culinary creation. This recipe is largely inspired by Binging with Babish

As seen on: Hungry, Hungry Homer (Season 12 Episode 15)

Ingredients:

  • Hot dogs and buns of your choosing (recipe makes enough for about 8 regular or 6 large dogs).

Jalapeño Relish:

  • 8 jalapeños chopped(just the green outer part of the pepper, for extra spice you can add seeds)
  • 2 cloves of garlic, finely minced
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon of Kosher salt
  • 1/4 cup of sugar

Mango Lime Salsa:

  • 2 mangos, peeled with flesh removed and roughly chopped
  • 1/2 red onion, finely chopped
  • Juice from 1/2 a lime
  • Chopped cilantro, as much as desired
  • Kosher salt, to taste
  • Black pepper, to taste

Mesquite Grilled Onions:

  • 1 large onion, cut into rings

Steps:

  1. Cook hot dogs however you choose (grilled, roasted, smoked, boiled).
  2. Make the relish: Combine the garlic and chopped jalapeño into a bowl. You can put them into the food processor to achieve a more even, finer chop. This should be about the consistency of your desired relish.
  3. In a sauce pan, mix vinegar, water, salt, and sugar and then bring to a boil, ensuring everything is dissolved and mixed. This will act as a quick-brine.
  4. Put jalapeño mixture into a heatproof bowl and pour the brine on top.
  5. Cook hot dogs however you choose (grilled, roasted, smoked, boiled).
  6. Let cool completely (about one hour) then drain off excess moisture and store in fridge till ready to use.
  7. Make the salsa: Take the chopped mango and put it into a bowl with the red onion, lime juice, cilantro, salt, and pepper.
  8. Mix it all together and store in the fridge till you're ready to use.
  9. Make the onions: If you have a charcoal grill, add some mesquite chips and grill the onions till there are nice grill marks on one side, flip and repeat (a few minutes per side).
  10. If you don't have mesquite chips you can either use a smoker set to 500°F or a regular grill.
  11. Chop up the grilled onions.
  12. Assemble: Put your cooked hot dog in a bun (toasted if you want), and top with the relish, salsa, and onions so they are next to each other in sections of the hot dog (see image above for reference).
  13. Enjoy!

P.S. don't forget to put it in a wrapper for the Albuquerque Isotopes.
