Red Velvet Cake

Image source: Preppy Kitchen

Description:

A classic cake, the red velvet cake traditionally got it's deep red color from the reaction of raw, non-Dutched cocoa powder reacting with the acidic ingredients such as buttermilk and vinegar. This recipe features a more modern approach which just uses food coloring to get that beautiful red color. A common pairing with red velvet cake is cream cheese frosting, but this recipe uses buttercream frosting instead. This recipe is borrowed from allrecipes.

Ingredients:

Cake:

  • 1 1/2 cups of white sugar
  • 1/2 cup of shortening
  • 2 eggs
  • 4 tablespoons of red food coloring
  • 2 tablespoons of cocoa powder
  • 1 cup of butter milk
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 2 1/2 cups of sifted all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon of distilled white vinegar
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons of baking soda

Icing:

  • 1 cup of milk
  • 5 tablespoons of all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup of white sugar
  • 1 cup of butter, room temperature
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract

Steps:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Grease two 9-inch round pans.
  2. Make the cake: Beat 1 1/2 cups of sugar and shortening together in a large bowl with an electric mixer until light and fluffy. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Combine red food coloring and cocoa powder to make a paste; add to creamed mixture.
  3. Mix buttermilk, salt, and 1 teaspoon of vanilla together in a small bowl. Add the flour, alternating with the buttermilk mixture, mixing just until incorporated.
  4. Mix the vinegar and baking soda together; gently fold into cake batter and pour into prepared pans.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean, about 30 minutes. Cool on a wire rack for 5 minutes. Run a table knife around the edges to loosen. Invert carefully onto a serving plate or cooling rack. Let cool, about 30 minutes.
  6. Make the icing: Heat milk and flour in a saucepan over low heat, stirring constantly, until thick. Set aside to cool completely.
  7. Meanwhile, beat sugar, butter, and vanilla together in a large bowl with an electric mixer until light and fluffy. Add cooled flour mixture and beat until frosting is a good spreading consistency. Frost cake layers when completely cool.
